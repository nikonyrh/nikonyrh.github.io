About me

Hi!

I am Niko, a Soft­ware En­gi­neer and an Ap­plied Math­emat­ics en­thu­si­ast. My main in­ter­ests are in ma­chine learn­ing, com­puter vi­sion, al­go­rithms, data struc­tures and multi-thread pro­gram­ming (in­clud­ing CUDA). I don't mind get­ting my hands dirty with C++ when high per­for­mance is re­quired, but usu­ally I find my­self writ­ing Mat­lab or PHP scripts.

I have al­ways done small pro­jects dur­ing my free time, and be­fore this blog I just had ran­dom scripts lay­ing on my hard disk and maybe some screen­shots in Drop­box. Thus I didn't have any easy way to share them with oth­ers. This mo­ti­vated me to write sim­ple blog ar­ti­cles, so I ended up writ­ing my own en­gine. It is based on Git for SCM and CMS, LaTeX for writing, PHP to generate navigation links etc. and mus­tache tem­plat­ing to generate the final HTML. Most of the server side functionalities can be avoided by utilizing 3rd party services such as Google An­alyt­ics and Dis­qus, own JavaScript implementations. To my surprise it took only about 1000 lines of PHP to get the basic stuff working such as tag-based browsing of articles and references to automatically numbered figures. More detailed info can be found from the Blog­ging plat­form article. Currently static output files are hosted at Github pages and optional PDF files are in Drop­box's public folder (HTTP 302 redirected from a micro AWS instance at cdn.nikonyrh.org).

In April 2014 I joined Anal­yse2's analytics team, where we (a few doctors and masters) focus on predictive modeling of product sales volumes of tens of thousands of products in thousands of stores. We might also build infrastructure for efficient ad-hoc requests on terabytes of ticket-level shopping data. These systems can be used to answer question on topics such as product promotions and assortment optimization.

Be­tween 2010 and 2013 I spent two years in Sin­ga­pore: a stu­dent ex­change pro­gram at the Na­tional Uni­ver­sity of Sin­ga­pore, and an in­tern­ship and full time po­si­tion at Prop­er­tyGuru. Ini­tially my main role was a PHP & MySQL de­vel­oper, but later it evolved to data anal­ysis in a newly formed team. The main fo­cus was on an au­to­mated prop­erty val­ua­tion al­go­rithm and to make it avail­able at REST­ful API. Its in­ter­face is pub­licly vis­ible at api.prop­er­tyguru.com (/v1/prop­er­ties/[id]/val­ua­tion/...), but queries aren't ac­cepted with­out valid OAuth au­then­ti­ca­tion.

I hope to keep this blog rea­son­ably ac­tive, but not all my pro­jects are worth the ef­fort of ac­tu­ally writ­ing an ar­ti­cle out of it. The in­ten­sity seems to be one ar­ti­cle ev­ery one or two months, so stay tuned! I hope to im­ple­ment proper RSS feeds some­day, add com­ments to this blog (via the Isso pro­ject) etc.
